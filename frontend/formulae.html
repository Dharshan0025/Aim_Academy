Essential Formulae Reference

Algebraic Identities

Square of a Sum
(a + b)² = a² + 2ab + b²
This identity shows how to expand the square of a binomial. It's fundamental for simplifying algebraic expressions and solving equations.

Example

(x + 3)² = x² + 2(x)(3) + 3² = x² + 6x + 9

Difference of Squares
a² - b² = (a + b)(a - b)
This identity is used for factoring expressions and simplifying complex fractions. It's particularly useful in solving quadratic equations.

Example

x² - 16 = (x + 4)(x - 4)

Quadratic Formula
x = [-b ± √(b² - 4ac)] / 2a
The solution to the quadratic equation ax² + bx + c = 0. The discriminant (b² - 4ac) determines the nature of the roots.

Example

For 2x² + 5x - 3 = 0, x = [-5 ± √(25 + 24)]/4 = [-5 ± 7]/4 ⇒ x = 0.5 or -3

Arithmetic and Geometric Progressions

AP nth Term
aₙ = a₁ + (n - 1)d
Arithmetic progression nth term formula, where a₁ is the first term and d is the common difference between terms.

Example

For AP: 3, 7, 11,... 10th term = 3 + (10-1)×4 = 39

AP Sum
Sₙ = n/2 [2a₁ + (n - 1)d] = n/2 (a₁ + aₙ)
Two equivalent forms for the sum of first n terms of an arithmetic progression.

Example

Sum of first 10 terms of 3,7,11,... = 10/2 (3 + 39) = 210

GP nth Term
aₙ = a₁ × rⁿ⁻¹
Geometric progression nth term formula, where a₁ is the first term and r is the common ratio between terms.

Example

For GP: 2, 6, 18,... 5th term = 2 × 3⁴ = 162

2D Shapes

Area of Rectangle
A = l × w
Area of a rectangle equals length multiplied by width. Fundamental for all area calculations.

Example

Rectangle with l=5cm, w=3cm has A=15cm²

Area of Circle
A = πr²
Area of a circle with radius r. π (pi) is approximately 3.14159.

Example

Circle with r=7cm has A≈153.94cm²

Area of Triangle
A = ½ × b × h
Area of a triangle equals half the product of base and height. Applies to all triangle types.

Example

Triangle with b=8cm, h=5cm has A=20cm²

3D Shapes

Volume of Cuboid
V = l × w × h
Volume of a rectangular prism (cuboid) equals length × width × height.

Example

Cuboid 4cm×5cm×6cm has V=120cm³

Volume of Sphere
V = (4/3)πr³
Volume of a sphere with radius r. Surface area is 4πr².

Example

Sphere with r=3cm has V≈113.1cm³

Basic Trigonometric Identities

Pythagorean Identity
sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
Fundamental identity relating sine and cosine functions of the same angle.

Example

If sinθ = 0.6, then cosθ = √(1 - 0.36) = 0.8

Tangent Identity
tanθ = sinθ / cosθ
Definition of tangent function in terms of sine and cosine.

Differentiation

Power Rule
d/dx(xⁿ) = nxⁿ⁻¹
Fundamental rule for differentiating polynomial terms.

Example

d/dx(x³) = 3x²

Financial Accounting

Accounting Equation
Assets = Liabilities + Owner's Equity
Fundamental equation that forms the basis of double-entry bookkeeping.
Gross Profit Margin
(Revenue - COGS) / Revenue × 100%
Measures how much out of every dollar of sales a company retains as gross profit.
